Pokemon Legends: Arceus

Pokemon Legends: Arceus (PLA) takes the general Pokemon formula we've come to know and love, crumples it into a nice paper ball and throws into the waste basket across the room.

PLA introduces a new Pokemon formula that is very reminiscent of Monster Hunter. There's a base village where you can pick up sidequests and check your equipment, whether it be you current party of Pokemon, your usable items, or simply your appearance. While there is no armor, per se, there is a clothing shop where you can change the hat, shirt, pants, and maybe one more article of clothing on your character. It doesn't have much depth to it actually.

Once you've decided to leave the village, you walk up to the gate and choose an area to go and explore. These areas are decently sized, though a bit barren, and just like Monster Hunter you can walk around and catch Pokemon you see to your heart's content. When you've had enough you can go back to one of the camps in the area and reqeust to go back, taking with you all the spoils you've collected, including Pokemon, crafting materials, and other collectables.

Overall the game is really worth it. It has Pikachu. There's all the incentive needed.

Tunic

A time-warp back to the old days where games included meaningful manuals. Half of your time in this game will be spent in the menus looking at maps, clues, and instructions in the in-game manual. The loop of this game is basically walk around, find a page of the manual, look at it, decipher what it's trying to tell you, go explore, find another page, open it, scratch your head a lot, and keep exploring. It's masterfully done I would say, and when you figure out the puzzle that the game is laying out in front of you, the sense of accomplishment is unlike any other rush of dopamine I've experienced in games. At least not in a long, long time.
